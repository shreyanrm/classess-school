02 — Typography

One sans, weight does the work

No serif in the system — a single grotesque carries everything, with weight and size doing the hierarchy. Light at large sizes is the editorial voice; medium and semibold run the interface. Mono and Caveat appear only on demand: mono for data and timestamps, Caveat for the one human mark in the margin. Serif stays available for a rare moment that genuinely needs it, never as a default.

03 — Space, shape, depth

European-spacey, sharp, flat

Whitespace is compositional, not just air — generous, but structural. Corners are sharp, with subtle rounding as the ceiling. There are no drop shadows anywhere; depth comes from hairlines, tonal surface steps, and frost on overlays.

12 — The laws

Hold these everywhere

Do
  • Keep the shell monochrome steel; let one vivid block carry meaning.
  • One vivid per surface — a card belongs to a single subject.
  • Reserve molten for the brand mark and the mastery ignite.
  • Separate surfaces with hairlines and tonal steps, not shadow.
  • Sharp corners (0–3px). Sentence case. Tabular numerics in mono.
  • Let whitespace compose the page — generous and structural.
Don't
  • No drop shadows. No glow. No gradients on product surfaces.
  • No two vivids competing in one view — that's the rainbow trap.
  • No full-radius pills or bubbly rounding (reads soft, not European).
  • No grey or cream backgrounds — whites stay warm-white.
  • No emoji or exclamation marks in product copy.
  • No real pricing in tech-facing mockups — use ₹X,XXX.
